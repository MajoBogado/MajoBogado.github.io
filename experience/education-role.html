In Education

I've had two experiences on the other side of the classroom, and I genuinely loved both.

At UCES, I was a Teaching Assistant for the Identity Systems Design subject, a fourth-year course, the last year of the degree, and one of the most complex assignments students face. What I enjoyed most wasn't explaining theory; it was helping students get unstuck. Being someone who had just finished the career myself meant I could offer a perspective that was close to theirs. I remembered what it felt like to be in that exact place, and that made the conversations more honest and useful.

At UBA, I co-coordinated a postgraduate specialization in Design and Brand Management. This one was less about teaching and more about making everything run, and there's a lot more to that than it sounds. Coordinating between professors and students, making sure everyone had what they needed and when they needed it, keeping the campus updated, tracking invoices from professors to the university, proposing tools and methods to make the admin side less chaotic.

I couldn't continue down this path because I decided to go back to studying myself, and between that and my full-time job there was simply no room left. But it's something I'd love to return to. If you have a proposal or opportunity in education, I'd genuinely love to hear from it — reach out on LinkedIn.
