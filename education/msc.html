Master's in Applications of Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare

Universidad a Distancia de Madrid (UDIMA)
· in collaboration with CEMP

Most people would assume the next step after a master's is a PhD. I thought so too, I genuinely considered it. But then life happens. Other interests emerge, circumstances shift, geography plays a role, and things get complicated.

Once again, I didn't go back to studying just for the sake of it.

The more I read and heard about AI (jobs being replaced, chaos, stocks swinging, everyone's going to be out of work) the more it made me think. Remember the article I wrote about AI for my Interactive Design master's? I'd already explored the topic, started using AI tools, formed an opinion, and decided to stay optimistic. But when the world keeps telling you something different, it gives you pause.

I decided to study AI and its applications in healthcare specifically, to understand what it really means to use AI in a field where a single error can have a profound impact on someone's life.

So I've been learning about regulations, privacy and data management, and data governance — from both an ethical and regulatory standpoint — but also diving into the technical side: exploring different types of AI algorithms and their use cases, the role of AI versus the role of the doctor or specialist, where responsibility lies, and what responsible use actually looks like.
