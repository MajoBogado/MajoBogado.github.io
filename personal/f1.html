Formula 1

I remember watching F1 as a kid with my dad. I must have been around 8. Honestly, I didn't get it, I found it boring. I'd count the laps waiting for it to end because, you know, there was one TV in the house and you had to wait for the race to finish before you could watch something else or play SEGA. Whoever behaved best got to pick what came next, and to play SEGA, yes, there were two controllers and three of us in line.

So no, I never understood or liked F1 — until I grew up and an Argentine driver, Franco Colapinto, gave us something to watch. Because that's just how we are in Argentina: the moment there's an Argentine out there doing something, we all show up (to cheer or to criticize, let's not pretend we're all that wholesome).

I reconnected with F1, but from a completely different place this time, trying to understand why Franco had basically no chance of scoring points in 2025 and why every race seemed to go sideways for him.

And somewhere in those races where we already knew Franco was starting 20th and probably finishing 20th, you start to notice the other drivers, to realize there are multiple races happening inside the same race, to pick your own race to follow and find your backup drivers. So every race became:

  • Hoping Franco finishing better than he started, beating his teammate, or honestly sometimes just "please finish the race".
  • Rooting for Verstappen.
  • Watching how long it takes one driver to overtake another.
  • Rooting for Fernando Alonso, who also didn't have the best car (and 2026 has been even worse).
  • Rooting for Hamilton too, who's having a better 2026 and it genuinely does good things for the sport.

I have a pending F1 project I want to build: something that answers the questions I find myself googling before and during every race.
